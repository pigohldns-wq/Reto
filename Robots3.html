Robot otto ninja

The Otto Ninja is a biped robot that walks on two legs and is controlled from a smartphone via Bluetooth. Its role in the competition is combat: two robots face each other in a bounded arena and the goal is to push the opponent out of the zone. We assembled it from scratch by 3D printing all its parts and programmed it with Arduino. The biggest challenge was calibrating its movement to be stable while walking and agile enough for combat.

Robot otto quad(spider)

The Spider is a hexapod robot, meaning it has six legs and walks like an insect. It is controlled from a smartphone via Bluetooth and competes in a sprint race: the first one to cover the set distance wins. It was one of the most complex robots to calibrate because coordinating six legs to achieve smooth movement requires a lot of fine tuning. We assembled it part by part and programmed each servo to achieve a stable and fast stride.

Robot BQ and line follower

The BQ Evolution is the robot we compete with in two different categories. On one hand, the obstacle circuit: the robot autonomously completes a course designed by the teachers while avoiding obstacles, and the one who finishes in the shortest time wins. For this we programmed it with a fixed timed route, calibrating each turn and each straight section with precise delay timings. On the other hand, the line follower: the robot autonomously follows a black line painted on the floor using two infrared sensors that detect when the robot drifts and correct the path in real time. Two competitions, one robot, two completely different programs.
