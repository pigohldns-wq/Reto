Who are we?

We are Barceló FC, a team from the SMR1 vocational training cycle at ALMI Center in Bilbao. We take part in Challenge 5 RobotALMI, a project where we have designed, assembled and programmed real robots to compete in a European robotics competition. Over 3 weeks we have worked on Arduino programming, hardware assembly, WiFi network security and web designing.

Our purpose?

Our purpose is to prove that with teamwork, dedication and a willingness to learn, real technology projects can be built from scratch. RobotALMI is not just a school challenge, it is our way of stepping into the professional world: assembling hardware, writing code, securing networks and building our own brand.

What we have done?

Over 3 week we have carried out a complete robotics project. We assembled three robots from scratch: the Otto Ninja, the BQ and the Spider. We programmed each one in Arduino with C++, creating algorithms for autonomous movement and Bluetooth control from a smartphone. We also designed and 3D printed parts for the robots. To protect communications during the competition, we set up our own secure WiFi network with WPA3 encryption. On top of that, we built this website in three languages, managed the code with GitHub and designed the team's brand identity.
